VARScent



overview

Exploratory work on bringing scent into VR for use in therapy for Alzheimers and dementia. Built with Unity C#, Arduino, and custom hardware.

my role

I got the opportunity to work with the Boise State VARScent team for the summer of my sophomore year of college. I worked as solo dev doing work in Unity C# and Arduino Code.

The team had already created an arduino solution to run their poprietary hardware, which could be attached to the front of a VR headset (shown above). What they did not have at this point was a way to integrate that hardware into a VR experience.

At this stage of my VR journey, I wasn't really an expert in what I was doing, so it took me some time to research possible solutions. I stumbled on a package for Unity called Ardity, which did 90% of what I needed. So I built an implementation using Ardity and re-wrote the existing Arduino code in order to make them work together.

Finally, I put together a little demo to show the non-software-aligned stakeholders what had been accomplished. At that point, I was just about out of summertime, so I took my leave from the group in order to continue learning in other areas.

what we did

VARScent is a very exploratory project on the Boise State campus and is not (at the time that I wrote this) putting foward any finished projects for the public to see. I'm not sure how much I can say about the inner workings of their hardware, so I'll err on the side of caution.

The stated goals of VARscent is to build tools for therapies for dementia and alzheimers. The ability to bring scent into VR is a powerful tool for these and other applications.

Altogether, working with the VARscent group was a great opportunity to work on a novel problem, learn a lot, and make something that perhaps hadn't been made before.
