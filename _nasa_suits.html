my role

I worked as an XR developer for half a year before being invited to replace the graduating student lead.

As student lead, I onboard new members, lead agile standups, create and maintain design documentation, lead the effort to better document our system for new members, maintain communication between stakeholders, and navigate bureaucratic hurdles from NASA and the University.

I also coordinated and wrote much of the research proposal that our group submitted to (and was accepted by) NASA earlier this year.

As an XR developer, I worked in Unity C# to take a 'minimap' navigation system from concept to completion. That is still my area of expertise and am spearheading an overhaul to it this year.

what we did

Here's a video overview: link

Boise State University has been a participant in the NASA SUITS Microgravity University Challenge for going on 5 years (at the time of this writing). The goal behind this challenge is to find new ways for NASA to use AR for space exploration -- particularly in the realm of EVAs.

Our system (ARSIS 5.0) focuses on navigation and information display.

We have built a variety of tools over the years, using experimental software like the Mixed Reality Toolkit (MRTK), Oculus Passthrough, and cloud anchors. In addition, we've been innovative in prototyping custom solutions, like 3D-printed lighting fixtures, AR/VR mission control networking, integrating videogame mechanics into AR spaces, and more.

Overall, I'm proud of the part I have gotten to play in this project, both in terms of guiding its direction and in terms of code I've written.
